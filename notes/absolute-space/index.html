Absolute space

Posted on Jul 1, 2021

The Cartesian body

  • motion of a body is a change of relation to the bodies that surround it.
  • concept of inertia
    • a body’s motion will be preserved unless caused to deviate from its path
    • so not a function of its spatial relations to other bodies but of its causal relation

The Newtonian body

  • a body’s motion is not a function of its spatial relations to other objects but rather of whether forces are impressed on it.
  • disagreement with Descartes:
    • a body’s true motion is not only a relative change or a perceptive change, it is a body’s change of position within space itself
    • this means a body’s motion of all types should be understood as absolute motion
    • therefore the mathematical idea of space must be shared amongst all objects in the universe, independent of any objects and their relations
      • this is the concept of absolute space
